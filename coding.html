Coding, often referred to as programming, is the process of creating instructions for computers to follow. These instructions, written in various programming languages like Python, Java, or C++, enable computers to perform specific tasks, from simple calculations to complex data processing. Coding is the backbone of modern technology, powering everything from websites and mobile apps to artificial intelligence and robotics. By learning to code, individuals can develop problem-solving skills and logical thinking, which are valuable in many fields beyond technology12.

The world of coding is vast and ever-evolving, offering endless opportunities for creativity and innovation. Beginners can start with simple projects, such as building a personal website or creating a basic game, and gradually move on to more complex applications. Resources like online tutorials, coding bootcamps, and community forums provide ample support for learners at all levels. As technology continues to advance, coding remains a crucial skill, opening doors to exciting careers and the ability to shape the future of digital experiences
